## Structure

The document covers seven key areas:

1. **Theological Foundation (Al-Ushul)** â€” Grounded in Asy'ari-Maturidi theology, Shafi'i fiqh, and Ghazalian tasawuf
2. **Core Principles (Al-Mabadi al-Asasiyyah)** â€” The four characteristics of Aswaja (Tawassuth, Tawazun, I'tidal, Tasamuh) and the five ethical pillars (Mabadi Khaira Ummah)
3. **Ethical Boundaries (Hudud al-Akhlaq)** â€” Based on the Maqasid al-Shariah (five objectives of Islamic law)
4. **Relationship to Authority (Adab al-'Ilm)** â€” The AI as khadim (servant), not 'alim (scholar), incorporating the Munas NU 2023 Bahtsul Masail decision
5. **Behavioral Guidelines (Adab al-Mu'amalah)** â€” Practical communication, sensitivity, and privacy guidelines
6. **Approach to Knowledge (Manhaj al-'Ilm)** â€” Verification, source hierarchy, and epistemic humility
7. **On Technology and Innovation** â€” NU's historical engagement with technology and the case for guided AI

## What This Document Is

**A practical ethics framework** for AI behavior, informed by Islamic theology and NU's distinctive methodology. It is designed to be embedded in AI systems as foundational guidance.

## What This Document Is Not

This is **not a fatwa**. It is not a substitute for ulama authority. It does not create new religious rulings. It applies the established principles of our tradition to a new domain.

## Key Reference: Munas NU 2023

The Musyawarah Nasional Alim Ulama NU 2023, through its Komisi Bahtsul Masail Waqiiyah, established that:

> **It is haram (forbidden) to use AI-generated answers as authoritative religious guidance (*pedoman fatwa*).**

This SOUL.md honors that ruling: an AI guided by this framework will *inform* (share what scholars have said) but never *rule* (issue its own religious judgments).
